## Your Data Protection Rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)

If you are a California resident, you are entitled to the following rights under the CCPA:

1. Right to Know: You have the right to request that we disclose certain information to you about our collection and use of your personal information over the past 12 months.
2. Right to Delete: You have the right to request that we delete any of your personal information that we collected from you and retained, subject to certain exceptions.
3. Right to Opt-Out of Sale: If we sell your personal information, you have the right to opt out of that sale. However, we do not sell personal information.
4. Right to Non-Discrimination: We will not discriminate against you for exercising any of your CCPA rights.

To exercise your rights under the CCPA, please submit a verifiable consumer request to us by either:

- Emailing us at [support@mixiecooking.com](mailto:support@mixiecooking.com)
- Visiting our website at www.mixiecooking.com and submitting a request through our contact form
  Only you, or a person registered with the California Secretary of State that you authorize to act on your behalf, may make a verifiable consumer request related to your personal information. You may also make a verifiable consumer request on behalf of your minor child.

You may only make a verifiable consumer request for access or data portability twice within a 12-month period. The verifiable consumer request must provide sufficient information that allows us to reasonably verify you are the person about whom we collected personal information or an authorized representative.

We will respond to a verifiable consumer request within forty-five (45) days of its receipt. If we require more time (up to 90 days), we will inform you of the reason and extension period in writing.
For more information about the CCPA, please visit the official California Legislative Information website.

## Children's Privacy

Mixie is committed to protecting the privacy of children and young users. Our services are intended for users of all ages, including those under 18. We take specific measures to protect younger users and comply with applicable laws.

**For Users Under 13:**

- We collect only limited information from users under 13, including: • First name • Username • Email address (with parental consent) • Profile picture (optional)
- These users do not have public profiles
- We obtain verifiable parental consent before collecting personal information from children under 13
- Parents can review, edit, request deletion of, and refuse further collection or use of their child's personal information
- **For Users 13-18:**
- We collect the same information as for adult users but with additional safeguards
- Default privacy settings for these users are more restrictive
- We provide age-appropriate privacy information and tools

**For Student Users (Future Service):**

- When providing services to schools or educational institutions:
- We collect and use student data only for educational purposes
- We never sell students' personal information
- We do not use student personal information for targeted advertising
- We comply with FERPA (Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act) and other applicable education privacy laws • Schools/parents can review, edit, or delete student information • We provide additional security measures for student accounts
- **Parental Rights: Parents or guardians of users under 18 can:**

- Access their child's personal information
- Request changes or deletion of their child's data
- Refuse further collection or use of their child's information
- Provide or revoke consent for specific features
  To exercise these rights or for any questions about children's privacy, please contact us at [support@mixiecooking.com](mailto:support@mixiecooking.com). Please note that we may need you to verify your identity before responding to such requests.

We regularly review and update our practices to enhance protection for our younger users. As we develop our student services, we will update this policy with more specific information about data handling practices for educational contexts.
